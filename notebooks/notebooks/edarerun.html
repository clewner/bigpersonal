Here we’ll create a Bayesian regression model where we assume the relationship between the next-day funded total return and DTS of a bond can be modeled with a StudentT distribution, where the mean of the distribution has a linear relation to DTS and the intercept is determined by the bond’s sector (partially pooled across sectors).

Formally:

\[y_i \sim \text{StudentT}(\nu,\ \alpha_{s(i)} + \beta \cdot \text{dts}_i,\ \sigma)\]

Priors: - beta (DTS coefficient): Normal(mean=0.001, sd=0.5) - alpha[sector]: Normal(mean=0, sd=0.05) - sigma (StudentT scale): Uniform(0, 0.2) - nu (degrees of freedom): Uniform(9, 12)

sector, categories = pd.factorize(cleandata["industry_sector"].to_pandas())

with pm.Model(coords={"figi":cleandata["id_figi"], "sectors":categories}) as model:
    y = pm.Data("next_day_funded_total_return", cleandata["next_day_funded_total_return"], dims="figi")
    x = pm.Data("dts", cleandata["dts"], dims="figi")

    alpha = pm.Normal("alpha", 0, 0.05, dims="sectors")
    beta = pm.Normal("beta", 0.001, 0.5)

    sigma = pm.Uniform("sigma", 0, 0.2)

    retrieved_alpha = alpha[sector]
    mu = retrieved_alpha + x * beta
    nu = pm.Uniform("nu", 9, 12)
    y_hat = pm.StudentT("y_hat", mu=mu, nu=nu, sigma=sigma, observed=y, dims="figi")
with model:
    idata=pm.sample(nuts_sampler="nutpie")
    idata=pm.sample_posterior_predictive(idata, extend_inferencedata=True)
    pm.compute_log_likelihood(idata)

The Hyper Prior


Now let’s introduce a hyper prior on our alphas. Rather than treating each sector alpha as completely distinct, we treat each of them as a sample from the same distribution. Our prior essentially nudges the alphas closer together, all toward the mean of their hyper prior’s distribution.




sector, categories = pd.factorize(cleandata["industry_sector"].to_pandas())

with pm.Model(coords={"figi":cleandata["id_figi"].to_numpy().ravel().tolist(), "sectors":categories}) as modelHyperPriors:
    y = pm.Data("next_day_funded_total_return", cleandata["next_day_funded_total_return"], dims="figi")
    x = pm.Data("dts", cleandata["dts"], dims="figi")

    alpha_mean = pm.Normal("alpha_mean", -0.0013, 0.0003)
    alpha_var = pm.Exponential("alpha_var", lam=1)

    alpha = pm.Normal("alpha", alpha_mean, alpha_var, dims="sectors")
    beta = pm.Normal("beta", 0.001, 0.5)

    sigma = pm.Uniform("sigma", 0, 0.2)
    nu = pm.Uniform("nu", 9, 12)
    retrieved_alpha = alpha[sector]
    mu = retrieved_alpha + x * beta
    y_hat = pm.StudentT("y_hat", mu=mu, nu=nu, sigma=sigma, observed=y, dims="figi")
